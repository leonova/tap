Reasons not to sleep with your smartphone

Written by Geraldo Klein

How many of us are guilty of crawling into bed with our smartphone in hand? Going to sleep with your smartphone is highly discouraged. Dr Munidasa Winslow touches on why.

If you find yourself blinking furiously and looking at the ceiling, trying to be nonchalant as you read this, you know this is you. Delving deeper, we hope you have considered what this habit is capable of doing to a marriage.

Or has your smartphone already replaced your wife or husband? Married couples: that’s a very important question to answer. The long-time psychiatrist shares why you should not go to sleep with your smartphone, citing invasion of lives as a big reason.

Usually, if someone is already accustomed to using their smartphone at the dinner table instead of connecting with his spouse, the deed tends to extend to the bedroom as well.

Dr Munidasa Winslow, a pioneer in addiction and impulse control disorders across Asia Pacific and founder of Winslow Clinic, gives smart advice on smartphone handling while in a marriage and tips on combating insomnia.

Being on your smartphone regularly will result in phone dependency. A CNN article published the signs of phone addiction:

  • The urge to pull out a mobile phone even when someone you're with is in the midst of a conversation with you.
  • Texting even while your child is telling you about his or her day at school, and realising later that you can't remember the details of what your son or daughter has said to you.
  • Having the vague feeling that something hasn't really happened until you post it to Facebook or Twitter.
  • Feeling isolated and anxious if you are offline for an extended period of time.

After a day's work, night time is usually when couples spend their quality time together. Instead of concentrating and focusing on each other, and asking about each other's day, couples who constantly spend their time at home on their smartphones would drift apart and eventually 'fall out of love'.
